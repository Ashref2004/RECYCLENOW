Mechanical Recycling

Mechanical recycling recovers waste materials through physical processes like shredding, washing, and melting without chemical changes. This cost-effective method preserves material structure and is ideal for plastics, metals, paper, and glass.

85% Energy Saved
60% Less Pollution

Chemical Recycling

Chemical recycling breaks down polymers into original monomers through processes like pyrolysis and depolymerization. This advanced method handles complex plastics that mechanical recycling can't process, creating virgin-quality materials.

  • Processes contaminated plastics
  • Creates high-quality outputs
  • Complements mechanical recycling

Biological Recycling

Biological recycling uses natural processes like composting and anaerobic digestion to break down organic waste. This method transforms food scraps and agricultural waste into nutrient-rich soil amendments and renewable biogas.

Collection

Separate organic waste from other materials

Decomposition

Microorganisms break down the material

Product

Nutrient-rich compost or biogas
